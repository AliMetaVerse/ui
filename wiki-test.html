‌Overview

‌Webpage & Login


  • Access the Webropol Survey & Reporting system either:

    • via www.webropol.com > choose your country from the dropdown menu in the top right > click the Log in button on the top right of the re-loaded webpage > this will take to the webpage below

    • or via https://new.webropolsurveys.com/Account/Login directly


  • If you have forgotten your password, use the “I forgot my password” link as shown below, or contact your administrator.




    ‌Survey Creation & Access

    ‌Creating a new survey


  • New surveys (and other available modules) can be created from the homepage by clicking the Create new button.



    ‌Saving a new survey


  • Enter a title for the survey (this is also your survey name; you can change the title once created)

  • Select the relevant folder to save the survey into

  • Select a preferred Layout Template

  • Click Create Survey


    ‌Opening existing surveys & reports


  • Clicking Surveys from the homepage (highlighted blue below).

  • Search for your survey by name (example ringed in orange below) or scroll through folder/survey lists



  • Clicking the survey name will open the Edit tab or clicking the number of responses will open the Reporting tab


    ‌Changing the Survey Title


    The survey title can be different from the survey name. To edit:

  • Click on the survey title

  • Overwrite the text

  • Click outside of the text box



    ‌Accessing useful information within the survey


    From any tab, click the information icon on the top right



    From the pop-up window, the following can be done:


  • Survey name can be changed

  • Survey ID can be seen

  • Survey folder can be easily accessed by hyperlink

  • Survey rights can be seen and edited (depending on permissions)

  • History log can be accessed


    ‌Survey and Reporting Tabs

    ‌The Edit tab



    Used to create and edit elements of a survey, including but not limited to questions, layout, rules (skip logic) and formatting.

    Survey Structure [left-hand side]:


  • Provides an overview of each page, question and item added

  • Can be used for quick access to questions or pages instead of scrolling

  • Can move, copy or delete objects in the survey

  • Can be used for quick access to survey rules (if applicable)


    ‌The Collect Answers tab



    Initially, the tab will display clickable buttons (image below):


  • Email Link - Private

  • Weblink - Public

  • SMS Link – Private



    See Survey Publishing for further details


    Once the survey is “published” or “open”, the tab will display an overview instead.


    From the Overview page:


  • Links can be closed by clicking the button next to each status line

  • Upcoming schedules (where applicable) can be viewed

  • Number of responses can be seen

  • All existing responses can be deleted by clicking Reset survey and delete answers.

    *Caution: Resetting survey is permanent and irreversible*


    ‌The Follow Up tab



    Once responses have been collected, this will show you:


  • stats for your survey, broken down into link type responses

  • the number of responses as a count and a percentage

  • copies of each email link sent

  • respondent statistics (separate tab)


    ‌The Reporting tab



    Used to create reporting metrics on the survey responses collected, and display these statistics using graphs, charts and tables. Used to edit elements including but not limited to filters, comparison, layout and formatting.


    ‌Common Question Types

    ‌Selection [Single Answer Only]



  • Only one answer option can be selected by respondents.

  • To enter text, click in the relevant text box and type.

  • Press the <Enter> key after typing to add a new option line.



  • Useful for adding disclaimers or approval request questions:


    ‌Multiselections [Multiple Answers Allowed]



  • Multiple answer options can be selected by respondents



    To enter text:


  • Click in the relevant text box and type.

  • Press the <Enter> key after typing to add a new option line.

    ‌Matrix [Scale Selection]



  • Used to measure the level of agreement with given statements, usually on a sliding slide or to rank items in decreasing order of importance.



    To enter text:


  • Click in the relevant text box and type.

  • Press the <Enter> key after typing to add a new option line.


    To add an explanation of scale range below the question title:


  • Click Question Settings from the Matrix question toolbar.

  • Tick the box next to Show question description.

  • Click Apply.

  • To enter text, click in the text box and type.




    ‌Open Ended [for free text answers]



  • Allows respondents to give opinions or explanations

  • Provides context to quantitative answers


    ‌Label/Description Text [for Information or Subheadings]




  • Used to add informational text as an introduction or explanation, to add a subheading to create sections or to add images to the survey.

    For text:


  • Click in the text box and type or copy & paste.



    For subheadings:


  • Type relevant text into box.

  • Click Question Settings.

  • Tick Show free text as subheading.

  • Click Apply.



    ‌Net Promoter Score [NPS]



  • Uses a scale system to measure (commonly) the likelihood of recommendation of a product or service.

  • By default, the scale is set to 0 to 10 with contrasting statements on either end.

  • Scale values are divided into three categories: promotors (9&10), detractors (0-6), and passive (7&8).

  • Contrasting statements at either end of the scale are labelled automatically – click & type to edit.


    ‌Dropdown Selection [Single Answer Only]



  • Only one answer option can be selected by respondents.

  • Provides options in a more compact form.


  • To enter text, click on +Add or edit multiple response options.



  • Enter the choices in the text field.

  • Click Apply.


    ‌Contact Form [Personal Data Collection]



  • Used to collect respondent information easily in the form of a table.

  • Fields provided as default. These can be edited.

  • See special fields from option settings. Email fields need to be set as email.


    ‌Common Functions [Surveys]

    ‌Adding Questions


    Click +Add question underneath each question or click on the Add Question button in the top menu.




    By default, the system will offer the 5 most common question types on each new page.


    ‌Adding multiple answer options


  • Click +Add or edit multiple options

  • Copy & Paste answer options into text box

  • Click Apply


    *Each option must be on a new line*


    ‌Adding text field after answer option


    To add a text box to an Other (please specify) option:


  • Click on the Add text field after option button.

  • Tick Add text field after option for writing own answer.

  • Tick the Mandatory checkbox (to ensure an answer if given).

  • Click Apply.



    ‌Adding N/A to Matrix Scale Questions


  • Click +Add or edit multiple rows or columns.

  • Select Use other matrix values.




    An additional text box with a blue border will appear (image below).


  • Add “N/A” as a new line at the end of existing values.

  • Click Apply to finish.



    *Any text option you add must be excluded from the report weighting*


    To do this:


  • Click on the Option settings cog wheel icon next to the “N/A” value.

  • Tick the box labelled Exclude option from report average.

  • Click Apply.



    ‌Making Questions Mandatory


  • Click on the relevant question.

  • Click the mandatory on/off toggle button.

  • Select Question is mandatory.



    Same process applies to making answer options mandatory, eg. in the contact form.


    ‌Adding Page Breaks


    Page breaks are advised for surveys with more than 8 questions on one page to keep the questionnaire respondent friendly.


    Click +Add page break underneath each question or click on the Add New Page

    button in the top menu.



    ‌Adding a logo


    To add a company logo:


  • Click the Layout button from the main toolbar.

  • Click on the Logo, background & size icon from the dropdown menu.

  • Click Survey logo to expand the options.

  • Tick the box next to Use image from Image Gallery.

  • Click the Browse Gallery button.

  • Search or scroll to the relevant image.

  • Hover over image and click to select.




    The logo will show on every page in the survey, in the location specified, unless the option

    Only show logo on 1st page is ticked.


    Remember to add the Image alt text (hover over the icon for info):


    ‌Changing the layout


    To change the survey background:


  • Click the Layout button from the main toolbar.

  • Click on the Logo, background & size icon from the dropdown menu.



  • Click Background colors & images.


    The background is divided into two formattable parts:


  • Survey (the section with the questions)

  • Page background (the border around the survey section)



    To change the colours:


  • Click on the white square next to the Color label under either Survey color or

    Page background

  • Choose a colour by dragging the tiny circle to the relevant spot in the colour square or by typing in the RGB colour code.

  • Click off the window to confirm selection.



    To add an image:


  • Tick the box next to Use survey background image from Image Gallery or Use page background image from Image Gallery.

  • Click Browse Gallery and choose an image.

  • Decide how or if the image should repeat, eg. Vertically and horizontally is the best fit for smaller images.

  • Decide if the image will be scaled to fit or not

  • Decide if the image will scroll or be fixed (for page images only)

  • Decide the image placement



    To set the transparency:


  • Select the No color (transparent) option (circled in blue below) or drag the slider on the right-hand side next to Set transparency to the desired percentage (circled in orange below).



    To adjust the widths, margins and alignments of the survey:


  • Click Survey size & alignments.

  • Adjust to desired requirements.

  • Click Apply.



    A toaster notification should appear if the upload works (the message will pop out from the side as per image below).


    ‌Text Editor



  • Used to edit text within the survey.

  • Click the icon to enable and disable.

  • If enabled, will pop up automatically when usable.


    *You must highlight any text you wish to change before using the Editor toolbar*


    ‌Adding hyperlinks


    To create a link to the policy for respondents to read, turn on the Text Editor if disabled and highlight the words you want to hyperlink. Then click the Link icon.



    Copy the URL address of your privacy statement and paste into the URL field in the link popup window. Select “https://” from the Protocol dropdown. Click OK.


    ‌Adding Jump Rules


    Skips the respondent forward to a specific section or question. To add rule to a question:


  • Click on the Jumps and Rules icon in the selected question.

  • Click +Add new rule under relevant option.

  • Leave If option is selected (default) for the first part of the condition.

  • Choose Jump to Page from the dropdown menu for the Select action.

  • Choose Background Information from the Select Questions dropdown.

  • Click Apply (if there is only one logic to add) or click the Save icon to the right of the rule you just added.







    *You must insert at least one page break before the question you want to jump to*


    If you have no page breaks, the Jump to Page option will be greyed out and you will not be able to select it.



    If you have page breaks after the question you want to jump to, you can select the Jump to Page but in the next dropdown, the questions before the page break will be greyed out and you won’t be able to choose them.



    *It is recommended to make jump questions mandatory to ensure respondents only see questions relevant to the answers they give*

    ‌Adding Show/Hide Rules


    Only reveals questions under certain criteria. To add rule to a question:


  • Click on the Jumps and Rules icon in the selected question.

  • Click +Add new rule under relevant option.

  • Leave If option is selected (default) for the first part of the condition.

  • Choose Show Question(s) from the dropdown menu for the Select action.

  • Choose applicable question(s) from the Select Questions dropdown.

  • Click Apply (if there is only one logic to add) or click the Save icon to the right of the rule you just added.



    A pop window will notify you that the chosen question(s) are now hidden.


    ‌Editing Jumps & Rules (Skip Logic)


    To access the rule(s) for editing (images below):


  • Click the link in the notification in an applicable question.

  • Click the Jump and Rules icon next to an applicable question in the Survey Structure (or in the question).

  • Click on Show survey rules in the Survey Structure.



    ‌Adding a Progress Bar


    A progress bar is useful to track how far through a survey you are, especially if it is a lengthy survey or you have page breaks.

    To add one:


  • Click Layout.

  • Click Progress Bar.

  • Choose Placement & Alignment.


    Choosing a placement option will open more settings:


  • Start progress bar/page numbering on second page

  • Choose whether to Show percentage and/or number of pages

  • Choose Text Placement.

  • Preview and format the progress bar in the Type and color section.

  • Click Apply.



    ‌Adding alternative languages


    Click on the Languages icon at the top of the screen:



    By default, the survey is set to match the language set in your user environment.


    To add additional languages, choose them from the dropdown menu. Select as many as needed. You can allow respondents to change the language during answering by ticking the checkbox on the right hand side.



    The default language will be marked with a star and languages can be deleted using the bin icons next to each one.

    To add language text by Excel upload, click on Translate alternative survey languages.


  • Select both your default and alternative language(s) from the dropdown list.

  • Click Get template file.


    *Always add the default language as well as the additional languages to the template to easily see what text needs to be translated*



    Once the file has downloaded:


  • Open it and click Enable Editing or save.

  • Copy the text in the white fields only.

  • Paste the translated version in the corresponding language column.

  • When done, save and close the Excel.



    In Webropol Survey and Reporting:


  • Under 2. Import Excel with ready translation, click Choose File.

  • Select your saved document.

  • Click Upload File.

  • Click Apply.



You should see the below toaster notification if the upload works.



The survey languages available will appear in a dropdown menu in the top right corner of the screen, in both the Edit and Reporting tabs.


‌Survey Settings



‌Anonymity


Sometimes it is necessary to hide the identity of the respondent, especially common in employee satisfaction surveys to conform to GDPR requirements.


*Setting a survey as anonymous must be done before it is published*


In the Settings menu:


  • Click on Anonymity.

  • Tick the box next to Collect answers anonymously to activate the other options.

  • The default option is Hide email addresses from responses and allow to see individual anonymous responses (possible to export responses per row in

    e.g. Excel export.

  • Click Apply.



    The Settings menu will alert you to the changed status of the survey:


    ‌Redirection


    You can redirect respondents from the thank you page to a specified network address (for example, a website homepage), to another survey or to a specified report to a survey.

    In the Settings menu:


  • Click on Forwarding.

  • Select the relevant forwarding type, eg. Webpage (URL).

  • Type/copy the web address into the text box or choose the relevant survey/event/report from the dropdown menus.

  • Adjust the number of seconds before forwarding if needed.

  • Click Apply.


    ‌Previewing and testing


    Surveys can be tested/previewed by clicking the Preview & test button, which will open in a new browser tab.



    Things that can be checked:


  • The scalability of the survey on other devices

  • Any question rules

  • Alternative languages


    A sample of the survey can be exported to PDF or Word.


    ‌Survey Publishing

    Once the survey has been tested and is ready for publishing:


  • Click the Collect answers tab at the top of the page.


    Initially, the tab will display clickable buttons (image below):


  • Email Link - Private

  • Weblink - Public

  • SMS Link – Private



    Creating links for your survey also publishes it and limits the amount of editing you can do, as follows:


    ‌Sending Private Links [Email]


    To use a private link, you will need the email address of the respondent:


  • Click on the Email Survey – Private Links button.

  • Click +Add recipients.

  • Under the Type heading, type (an) email address(es) into the text field.

  • Click the Add recipients button.




    To upload a spreadsheet of recipients:


  • Click +Add recipients.

  • Click on the Excel tab.

  • Click Export recipients Excel template.


  • Save the file that opens

  • Enter the data (one recipient per row).

  • Close Excel once finished



    *Putting data in the Mobile field will automatically add a SMS recipient*


    Return to the Survey & Reporting software to upload:


  • Click on Choose File under 2. Import recipients from excel file.

  • Select the relevant spreadsheet.

  • Click Add recipients.



    To send the survey:


  • Scroll down to the Send Email Manually tab.

  • Click on the To field.

  • Choose applicable option from the dropdown menu, eg. Select recipients from list.

  • Choose an Email template or fill in Subject and message in email body.


  • Preview email message if needed.

  • Click Send survey.



    Once you click Send survey, you should see the following popup message:


    ‌Sending Public Links [Weblink]


    You can also collect answers using a public weblink. This can be shared, for example, to a website homepage or on social media.

    Respondents to a public link cannot be identified unless a contact form is used in the survey. The generic link is the same for everyone.

    From the Weblink – Public Link page:


  • Click Open Weblink Now.

  • Click View in browser to test it works (opens the survey in new window).



    ‌Sending Private Links [SMS]


    SMS survey links work in tandem with the email option.


    *Sending SMS messages requires credits – contact Helpdesk for more info*


    To use a private link, you will need the phone number of the respondent:


  • Click on the SMS Survey – Private Link button.

  • Click +Add recipients.

  • Click the Type heading.

  • Type (an) email address(es) into the text field.

  • Click the Add recipients button.




    To upload a spreadsheet of recipients:


  • Click +Add recipients.

  • Under the Excel tab, click Export recipients Excel template.


  • Save the file that opens

  • Enter the data (one recipient per row).

  • Close Excel once finished



    *Putting data in the Email field will automatically add an Email recipient*


    Return to the Survey & Reporting software to upload:


  • Click on Choose File under 2. Import recipients from excel file.

  • Select the relevant spreadsheet.

  • Click Add recipients.



    To send the survey:


  • Scroll down to the Send SMS messages tab.

  • Click on the Send to field.

  • Choose applicable option from the dropdown menu.

  • Type a message (be aware of characters limits and costs!).

  • Check Include link to survey is ticked.

  • Click Apply.




    ‌Editing or deleting recipients


    To edit individual rows in the recipient table:


  • Click on the pencil icon next to the recipient.

  • Add in applicable data.

  • Click Apply.




    *Putting data in the Mobile phone field will automatically add a SMS recipient*


    To delete recipients:


  • Tick the lines to be removed.

  • Click Delete.


    ‌Scheduling


    Emails:


  • Click the Schedule (Forthcoming Schedules: No) tab.

  • Click the Create schedules button (a dropdown list will give you options).



    For all options:


  • Select the date and time you wish to send the email.

  • For email-based options (send survey, send reminder, send thank you), setup the message & recipient settings.

  • Click Create Schedule.



    Weblink:


  • Click Schedule Survey Weblink

  • Choose either Open Weblink or Close Weblink as applicable.

  • Select Frequency, Date & Time.

  • Click Create Schedule.




    ‌Common Functions [Reporting]

    ‌Accessing Reports


    Access all the reports saved for your survey by:


  • Clicking the Reporting tab

  • Clicking the dropdown button next to the Basic report title in the top left corner.



By default, the basic report is only one created automatically and it cannot be renamed or deleted, only copied.

‌Basic Report [Editing & Saving Changes]


To save changes:


  1. Underneath the Basic report field, click Save as.



  2. Click the dropdown (1) and then click the Copy icon next to the basic report name in the list (2).



  3. Type “Custom Report 1” into the text box and click Save.



    Once you alter something within the report, the system will log an unsaved, edited version:



    ‌Respondents view



    *If survey is anonymous, no personal data will be available in this table*


    If survey not anonymous:


    • Individual respondent answers can be identified

    • Individual respondent reports can be created

    • Respondent data or answers can be edited or deleted


      ‌Changing the chart types


      Reports will display the charts based on the question types in use. For example, selection questions are always presented as percentage bar graphs and matrix questions as average score bar graphs.

      To change these by question type:


    • Click the Layout button from the main toolbar.

    • Click the Report’s General settings and Chart Types option.

    • Toggle show/hide settings for whole report using General settings for whole report.

    • Choose the question type grouping to be edited, eg. Selection, Dropdown, Picture Selection.

    • Work down the option, clicking to open each section, eg. changing bar chart to column, changing axis settings & display from percentage to count, and showing/hiding chart/table elements.

    • Click Apply.







      ‌Adding survey logo


      To include a company logo from the survey in the reports:


    • Click the Layout button from the main toolbar.

    • Click Report logo.

    • Click Show logo.

    • Click Apply.




      ‌Reordering report questions


      To reorder or hide questions in the report:


    • Click the Layout button from the main toolbar.

    • Click Question order and visibility.

    • Tick the boxes as applicable.

    • Move the question order using the eight dots icon.

    • Click Apply.



      ‌Saving and using layout templates


      Save layouts as templates to be used for new surveys or reports:


    • Click Save (this) layout as template.

    • Give the template an easily recognisable name.

    • Choose whether to save for your own use or share it with your user environment.

    • Click Save.




      Applying saved layout templates:


      For surveys:


    • Click Use (layout) template.

    • Select your own template (if applicable), a shared template, or a system template.

    • Click Preview selected template.

    • Click Apply this layout template.



      For reports:


    • Click Use layout template.

    • Select template from the list.

    • Click Apply selected template.



      ‌Formatting options on the graphs/charts



      From left to right:


    • Axis – change the axis values (count, %, average, etc).

    • Chart – change the chart values to match the axis values, as well as position the text within the chart.

    • Sort – clicking will cycle through descending, ascending and same-as-survey (which is default).

    • Swap axis – this is a two-option toggle button (clicking this once should show the legend and colour options).

    • Axis min & max – manually set the axis range if needed. It is automatically scaled by default.

    • Change size of graph – adjust graph height & width


      ‌Timeline



    • Used to show responses during specified time periods.

    • Best done from the basic report.

    • Best results from a survey that has a longer run time.


    • Choose question to be filtered.

    • Choose a start and end date.

    • Change the Show responses on chart dropdown as applicable.

    • Click Apply.


      ‌Response Rate


      The response rate displays a count and percentage of recipients and respondents (by private link only).

      It can be added before or after any question (though the data is always for the survey as a whole).

    • Click Add Elements.

    • Click Response rates.

    • Choose where to place.

    • Click Apply.



      By default, only the response rate percentage is shown but can be formatted as following:


    • Add border and/or colours by clicking on the response rate and choosing from the options

    • Click the settings cogwheel > show/hide chart, table, etc and then choose from the options available > click save once done



      ‌Contents menu


      Displayed as a numbered list at the top of the report by default, with hyperlinks to jump to each question/element.



      To add:


    • Click Add Elements.

    • Click Contents menu.

    • Choose where to place.

    • Choose which questions to include.

    • Click Create.


      ‌Report Publishing

      Click the Export & Share button from the main toolbar.



      ‌VIEW [Dashboard module]


      If you have a saved customised report open, you will be able to select the option to share to VIEW.



      Can be shared:


    • To individual users

    • To user groups

    • To recipients groups

    • By answer option filters

    • To an uploaded hierarchy


      ‌PDF, Microsoft Office and SPSS


    • PDF – recommended for distribution (either by email attachment or printing).

    • PowerPoint – recommended for presenting the data.

    • Word – useful for commenting or writing a report on the results.

    • Excel – useful for further analysis or chart/graph editing.

    • SPSS – for use with IBM SPSS Statistics software.




      ‌Public Link


    • To create a public link, click Share report[s] as a public link.

    • To access existing links, click Show public report links.



      You can set a password to restrict access to the public link, which is recommended if you are embedding the link into a website, or if the survey contains confidential information.



      ‌Report Export List


      When you click Export, you will get two toaster notifications. Click the orange hyperlink to access the report.




      Alternatively, click Exported reports.



      ‌Scheduling



      To create a new schedule:


    • Select the report name, format, and frequency from the three dropdown menus.

    • Select the date(s) and time(s) you want the report to be sent.

    • Setup email message settings.

    • Click Apply.



      To access any existing saved schedules:


    • Re-open Schedule sharing of reports.

    • Click the All Saved Report Schedules tab (it should now have a 1 in brackets at the end).
