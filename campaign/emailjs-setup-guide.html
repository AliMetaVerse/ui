EmailJS Setup Guide for Email Campaign Tool

This guide will help you set up EmailJS to send real emails with the Email Campaign Tool. Follow these steps carefully to ensure everything works correctly.

Important: You must complete all these steps before attempting to send real emails through the campaign tool.

Step 1: Create an EmailJS Account

  1. Go to emailjs.com and sign up for a free account
  2. Verify your email address by clicking the link in the confirmation email
  3. Log in to your EmailJS dashboard

Step 2: Connect an Email Service

  1. In your EmailJS dashboard, click on "Email Services" in the left sidebar
  2. Click "Add New Service"
  3. Select an email provider (Gmail, Outlook, or another service you use)
  4. Follow the instructions to connect your email account
  5. Name your service (e.g., "Webropol Newsletter")
  6. After connection, you'll get a Service ID that looks like: service_xxxxxxxx
  7. Copy this Service ID - you'll need it for the campaign tool configuration

Step 3: Create an Email Template

  1. In your EmailJS dashboard, click on "Email Templates" in the left sidebar
  2. Click "Create New Template"
  3. Name your template (e.g., "template_newsletter")
  4. In the template editor, make sure to include these variables:
Variable Description Required
{{to_email}} Recipient's email address ✓
{{from_name}} Sender's name ✓
{{from_email}} Sender's email address ✓
{{subject}} Email subject line ✓
{{message_html}} The HTML content of your email ✓
{{campaign_name}} Name of the campaign (optional)

After saving the template, you'll get a Template ID that looks like: template_xxxxxxxx

Copy this Template ID - you'll need it for the campaign tool configuration

Step 4: Get Your Public Key

  1. In your EmailJS dashboard, click on "Account" in the left sidebar
  2. Look for "API Keys" section
  3. Copy your Public Key

Step 5: Configure the Email Campaign Tool

  1. In the Email Campaign Tool, click "Email Settings"
  2. Select "EmailJS" as your email service
  3. Fill in the following details:
    • Service ID: The service ID you copied in Step 2 (e.g., service_6t8hyif)
    • Template ID: The template ID you copied in Step 3 (e.g., template_newsletter)
    • Public Key: The public key you copied in Step 4
    • From Email: Your email address (e.g., ali.zuh.fin@gmail.com)
    • From Name: The sender name (e.g., Webropol Newsletter)
  4. Click "Save Settings"
  5. Optional: Click "Test Connection" to verify your configuration works

Troubleshooting

Common Issues:

  1. Emails not being sent
    • Check if your Service ID, Template ID, and Public Key are correct
    • Verify that your email service (Gmail, etc.) is properly connected
    • Check if you have the required template variables
  2. Error: "Email service is not properly configured"
    • Make sure you've completed all steps in this guide
    • Try clearing localStorage and re-entering your configuration
  3. Emails going to spam folder
    • Make sure your sender email is verified
    • Ask recipients to mark your emails as "not spam"
Remember: The free plan of EmailJS has limitations (200 emails per month). For large campaigns, consider upgrading to a paid plan.

Advanced Tips:

  • Use the browser console to check for errors (press F12)
  • Use EmailJS dashboard to monitor your email sending activity
  • For better deliverability, consider setting up SPF and DKIM records if you're using a custom domain

Troubleshooting with the Debug Tool

If you're having issues with EmailJS, the Campaign Tool includes a built-in debugging utility:

  1. In the Campaign Tool, click the Debug button (next to "Setup Guide")
  2. Use the Diagnostics tab to run tests on your EmailJS configuration
  3. Check the Logs tab to see detailed information about email sending attempts
  4. Use the Configuration tab to reset or repair your EmailJS settings if needed

The debug tool can identify common issues like missing configuration values, initialization problems, and template errors.
