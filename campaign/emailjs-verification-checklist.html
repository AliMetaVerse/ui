EmailJS Verification Checklist

Complete this step-by-step verification process to ensure your EmailJS integration is working properly. Check each item once completed.

1. EmailJS Account Setup

Create and verify your EmailJS account at emailjs.com.

Verification: You can log in to your EmailJS dashboard.

2. Email Service Connected

Connect an email service provider (Gmail, Outlook, etc.) to your EmailJS account.

Verification: In your EmailJS dashboard, you see your email service listed under "Email Services" with a green "Connected" status.

Service ID: Copy your Service ID (looks like "service_xxxxxxxx")

Your Service ID:

3. Email Template Created

Create an email template in your EmailJS dashboard that includes all required variables.

Required variables:

  • {{to_email}} - Recipient's email
  • {{from_name}} - Sender's name
  • {{from_email}} - Sender's email
  • {{subject}} - Email subject
  • {{message_html}} - Email content

Verification: You have created a template with all required variables.

Template ID: Copy your Template ID (looks like "template_xxxxxxxx")

Your Template ID:

4. Public Key Obtained

Get your public API key from your EmailJS account settings.

Verification: You have copied your Public Key from the "API Keys" section in your Account settings.

Your Public Key:

5. Campaign Tool Configuration

Enter your EmailJS details in the Email Campaign Sender tool.

Steps:

  1. Click "Email Settings" in the tool
  2. Select "EmailJS" as the service
  3. Enter your Service ID, Template ID, and Public Key
  4. Enter your From Email and From Name
  5. Click "Save Settings"

Verification: Settings are saved without any error messages.

6. Connection Test

Test the EmailJS connection from the debugging panel.

Steps:

  1. Click the "Debug" button in the tool
  2. Go to the "Diagnostics" tab
  3. Click "Run Diagnostics"

Verification: All items show green checkmarks, especially "EmailJS Initialization".

7. Send Test Email

Send a test email to yourself to verify the entire system is working.

Steps:

  1. Create a simple email in the campaign tool
  2. Click "Send Test Email"
  3. Enter your own email address
  4. Click "Send"

Verification: You receive the test email in your inbox.

Common Problems and Solutions

  • EmailJS SDK not loaded: Try refreshing the page or checking your internet connection.
  • Emails not being sent: Make sure your template variables exactly match what's expected ({{to_email}}, {{from_name}}, etc.)
  • Error messages: Copy the exact error message and search for it in the EmailJS documentation or contact their support.

Important Notes

  • The free EmailJS plan limits you to 200 emails per month.
  • Email delivery can sometimes be delayed by email servers.
  • Always test thoroughly before sending to a large audience.
